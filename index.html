Warm Up & Cool Down

Dynamic Warm-Up: Start with 5-10 minutes of light cardio (jogging, jumping jacks) and mobility drills to increase blood flow and prep your joints.

Cool Down: End every session with static stretching, focusing on major muscle groups. This helps reduce soreness and improve flexibility.

  • Never skip your warm-up or cool-down!
  • Try foam rolling for extra recovery.
  • Use FitTrack to log your warm-up and cool-down routines.

Track Your Progress: Use FitTrack to monitor your warm-up and cool-down routines, ensuring you stay consistent and effective.

Progressive Overload

Increase Intensity: Add weight, reps, or sets gradually to challenge your muscles and avoid plateaus.

Track Progress: Use FitTrack to log your workouts and see your improvements over time.

  • Change only one variable at a time (weight, reps, or sets).
  • Listen to your body and avoid overtraining.
  • Set realistic goals and celebrate small wins!

Track Your Progress: Use FitTrack to monitor your performance and adjust your training plan as needed.

Variety & Balance

Mix Workouts: Try different training styles (strength, HIIT, yoga, cardio) to keep things interesting and well-rounded.

Prevent Plateaus: Change your routine every 4-6 weeks to keep progressing.

  • Join group classes or try new activities for fun!
  • Balance your routine with strength, cardio, and flexibility work.
  • Use FitTrack to plan and track your varied workouts.

Track Your Progress: Use FitTrack to monitor your performance and adjust your training plan as needed.

Rest & Recovery

Listen to Your Body: Take at least 1-2 rest days per week. Quality sleep is essential for muscle repair and growth.

Active Recovery: On rest days, do light activities like walking, stretching, or yoga.

  • Use FitTrack to schedule rest and recovery days.
  • Consider foam rolling or massage for muscle soreness.
  • Stay hydrated and maintain a balanced diet to support recovery.

Track Your Progress: Use FitTrack to monitor your recovery and adjust your training plan as needed.

Nutrition Advice

Fuel your body with the right nutrients. These nutrition tips will help you eat smarter, feel better, and reach your goals.

Balanced Meals

  • Fill half your plate with vegetables and fruits.
Hydration

  • Stay hydrated before, during, and after workouts.
Meal Prep

  • Listen to your body’s hunger and fullness cues.

Balanced Meals

Balance is key: Each meal should have lean protein, complex carbs, healthy fats, and plenty of veggies.

  • Try the "plate method": 1/2 veggies, 1/4 protein, 1/4 carbs.
  • Limit processed foods and added sugars.
  • Use FitTrack to log your meals and track your nutrient intake.

Track Your Progress: Use FitTrack to monitor your meals and ensure you're getting a balanced diet.

Hydration

Stay hydrated: Water is essential for energy, digestion, and performance.

  • Carry a water bottle and sip throughout the day.
  • Limit sugary drinks and sodas.
  • Use FitTrack to log your water intake and stay accountable.

Track Your Progress: Use FitTrack to monitor your hydration levels and ensure you're drinking enough water daily.

Meal Prep

Plan ahead: Prepping meals saves time and helps you make healthier choices.

  • Batch cook proteins and grains for the week.
  • Use containers to portion meals and snacks.
  • Incorporate a variety of foods to keep meals interesting.

Track Your Progress: Use FitTrack to log your meal prep and ensure you're eating balanced meals throughout the week.

Mindful Eating

Be present: Eat slowly, enjoy your food, and avoid distractions like screens.

  • Pause between bites and check your hunger/fullness.
  • Practice gratitude for your meals.
  • Listen to your body’s signals to avoid overeating.

Track Your Progress: Use FitTrack to log your meals and reflect on your eating habits, helping you develop a healthier relationship with food.
